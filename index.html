Intro

I am a final-year undergraduate student in the Department of Automation at the College of Computer and Information Science, Southwest University. And now I'm also a visiting student in Westlake University, advised by Prof. Donglin Wang. In the past two years, I have served as a research assistant in the Artificial Intelligence and Big Data Laboratory, working on legal judgment documents advised by Professor Li Li. Later, under the guidance of Associate Professor Le Yin, I worked on the electronic design of autonomous vehicles and related tasks involving ROS and SLAM in the Electronic Design Innovation Laboratory.

My research interests are primarily focused on Embodied Artificial Intelligence and Computer Vision. My research interests focus on how to achieve broad generalization capabilities for open-world manipulation tasks, the application of Vision-Language-Action models to robots, and inference acceleration.

In my spare time, I have also gradually been paying attention to the application of AI in the medical field. Although this is not my primary research focus, from my personal experience, I hope that AI can play a crucial role in this area. I believe that technology should serve the people, which is the expectation that drives me—just as it has in my choice to research embodied intelligence.
