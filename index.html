Intro

Jia Bofang is an undergraduate student in the Department of Automation at the College of Computer and Information Science, Southwest University. He has served as an undergraduate research assistant in the Artificial Intelligence and Big Data Laboratory at Southwest University and has worked on autonomous vehicles related to ROS and SLAM in the Electronic Design Innovation Laboratory.

My research interests are primarily focused on robotics learning and embodied intelligence. I concentrate on how to achieve broad generalization capabilities and robust operations for open-world manipulation tasks. This involves enabling robots to operate beyond a specific environment, achieving broad generalization and robust operations, and is dedicated to perception and grasping tasks in robotics.

I am passionate about interdisciplinary research and enjoy thinking about and solving real-world problems. I believe that through the development of robotic technology, combined with knowledge from deep learning, robot learning, reinforcement learning, and control engineering, we can advance technology in fields such as healthcare, education, and home environments, bringing tangible benefits to society. This also contributes to the realization of AGI, which is a lifelong aspiration for me. I hope to make the most of my time and progress together with others in this field.

    I have some experience in designing and implementing robotic systems. Here are some of the robotic systems I have helped develop:
